Staying safe online as a college student is all about building strong digital habits. Think of your online identity as a high-security vault—you wouldn't leave the door unlocked, so why treat your data any differently?
Master Your Credentials
Start with your passwords. A strong, unique password is at least 12 characters long and uses a mix of letters, numbers, and symbols. Instead of trying to remember dozens of complex strings, use a reputable password manager to do the heavy lifting. To add an extra layer of protection, enable Two-Factor Authentication (2FA) on your most sensitive accounts like your email and college portal. This way, even if someone steals your password, they can't get in without your physical device. [1, 2, 3, 4] 
Stay Alert for Scams
Phishing is the most common way students lose their data. Always double-check a sender's email address and the website URL before you type in any login details. Look for "https://" and ensure the domain is exactly correct—for example, sbi.co.in is the official site, while something like "sbi-support.in" is a trap. Most importantly, never share your OTP with anyone. No legitimate bank or government official will ever ask for it over a call or text. [1, 5] 
UPI and Financial Safety
When using UPI apps, remember one golden rule: you only scan a QR code to send money, never to receive it. If someone asks you to scan a code for a "refund" or "prize," it’s a scam. You can also protect your bank account by setting a daily UPI transaction limit of ₹5,000 in your banking app.
What to do in an Emergency
If you ever fall victim to a cybercrime, don’t panic. Immediately call the National Cyber Crime Helpline at 1930 or file a report at the National Cyber Crime Reporting Portal. Acting fast is the best way to recover your funds.
