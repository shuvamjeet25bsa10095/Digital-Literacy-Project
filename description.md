Task 1 – Digital Literacy Awareness Infographic

Tools Used in the project
Canva

What the Infographic contains

The infographic is titled as "Be Digitally Smart: A Student's Guide" and covers the following topics:

1. What is Digital Literacy? — The ability to use digital tools effectively, responsibly, and safely.
2. Useful Digital Tools for Students — Google Workspace, GitHub, Canva, Notion, and HackerRank.
3. Safe Internet Practices — Using strong passwords, enabling 2FA, avoiding phishing links.
4. Professional Online Presence — Keeping LinkedIn and GitHub updated, maintaining a clean social media profile.
5. Email Etiquette — Writing clear subject lines, using professional greetings, and proofreading before sending.

Design Description

The infographic uses a very clean green-and-white color scheme with  pointer sections for easy reading. Each topic is presented in a separate  colour layout with a short heading.

Reflection

For this task, I used Canva to design a one-page digital literacy infographic aimed at first-year B.Tech students. Canva was an excellent choice because it offers hundreds of free infographic templates that require no prior design experience which aligned perfectly with the task's requirement of being browser-based and beginner-friendly.

My infographic covers five core topics: the definition of digital literacy, student-friendly digital tools, safe internet practices, maintaining a professional online presence, and email etiquette. I structured it as a pointer based layout so that each topic is visually distinct and scannable at a glance. I chose colours to represent each section because visual cues help readers quickly associate content areas without reading every word.

The most interesting challenge I encountered was deciding what to not include. Digital literacy is a very broad topic, and fitting meaningful content into a single page required making hard selectioned decisions. I had to select important topics to maintain clarity. This taught me that good design is as much about selective creativity a lesson that will also apply to academic presentations and professional communication as well.
