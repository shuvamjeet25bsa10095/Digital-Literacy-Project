A Closer Look at the Tasks
Task 1 – Designing for Awareness
I used Canva to create a one-page infographic. It’s a quick guide to what digital literacy actually looks like—covering everything from staying safe online to building a professional presence and using the right tools for college.
Task 2 – My Digital Identity
I officially set up my professional footprint on GitHub, LinkedIn, and Stack Overflow. I also wrote a custom GitHub README that shares a bit about who I am, my branch, and what I’m hoping to learn this year.
Task 3 – Testing the Waters with Code
I tackled my first challenge on HackerRank (Solve Me First) and built a custom Digital Literacy Quiz using Google Forms. It’s a 5-question check-in to see how much people know, and I’ve got it all linked to a Google Sheet to track the results.
Google Form (Digital Literacy Quiz): [https://forms.gle/SbF1Yh3RsTFg2QCe7] 
Task 4 – Masterful Communication
Communication is key, so I drafted two formal emails—one for a deadline extension and one for an internship inquiry. I also put together a "Social Media Do’s and Don’ts" list specifically for college students to help us keep our feeds professional.
Task 5 – Staying Safe in a Digital World
I took a deep dive into Phishing, researching a real-world case study and writing a 250-word breakdown. To wrap it up, I created a "Stay Safe Online" checklist with 8 practical tips, including a few specifically for UPI and digital payments.
